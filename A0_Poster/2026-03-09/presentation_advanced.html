Backend-Dependent Stability on Legacy HBM GPUs

Advanced Evidence Edition: ROCm/HIP vs Vulkan on Vega/gfx900

Akira Ito | AETS (Akatsuki Enterprise Technology Solutions) | aets-giken@hiroshima-aktk.com

IEICE GNW-68 | Kyushu Sangyo University | March 9, 2026

1

Background & Motivation

  • In ROCm 7.2, gfx900 is not listed in the official support matrix; however, this local environment still showed executable residual paths and shipped artifacts.
  • For students and small labs, the key question is whether execution is possible, stable, and reproducible.
  • We built a matched dual-backend testbed on the same Vega device to separate support policy, backend choice, and runtime behavior.
  • Goal: explain why "unsupported" and "unusable" are not the same statement.
2

Problem Statement

  • Legacy Vega GPUs are often treated as categorically unsuitable for modern ROCm workloads.
  • Success or failure may be decided at different layers: distribution presets, build targets, init validation, backend macros, or runtime compute paths.
  • We ask: is instability caused by Vega itself, or by a specific backend path under matched conditions?

Engineering Challenges

  • gfx900 is not listed in the ROCm 7.2 official support matrix, yet residual build/runtime paths remain in this environment.
  • `num_gpu` is often misread as device count, causing wrong failure attribution.
  • Under matched conditions, the same Vega hardware shows different outcomes across backends.
  • Diagnosis requires layered evidence (source, logs, run artifacts), not single-run anecdotes.

Why gfx900 Still Works Sometimes

ROCm changelog wording is closer to "no longer built by default" than "forbidden." This local install includes gfx900 hsaco artifacts and libggml-hip.so references. Within this evidence scope, official non-listing indicates unguaranteed/untested status, not immediate impossibility.

Limitations & Future Work

Limitations: Current matched comparison is one-epoch and single-model (qwen3.5:2b). Vulkan/ROCm versions differ (0.17.4 vs 0.17.5). tinyllama behaved differently from qwen3.5 on Vulkan.

Future work: Multi-epoch stress runs, more models (llm-jp, phi-3), ROCm 6.x vs 7.x comparison, and GGML_CUDA_NO_PEER_COPY experiments to isolate compute-path failure conditions.

10

Takeaway & Key Messages

Support Status vs Execution Reality

Official non-listing does not automatically imply non-execution. In our gate analysis, five of six examined layers retained paths that may allow gfx900 execution in this environment.

Backend Choice Matters Under Matched Conditions

On identical hardware and workload, ROCm/HIP passed all tested num_gpu cases, while Vulkan failed for num_gpu>=1 in the tested runs.

Evidence Before Claims

Every claim is linked to run_id + file path + line-level code trace for reproducibility.

Within this tested scope: non-listed does not always mean unusable. | Backend-dependent behavior matters.
